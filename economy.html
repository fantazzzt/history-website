Economic Decline

Spanning 284-750 CE

One of the most important conclusions that recent historical analysis on Late Antiquity has been that the period is not one of sole decline. While political integrity of Western Roman bureaucratic institutions had been atrophied, cultural and religious transformations were important developments that contradict the notion of a general decline. Partially, this can be applied to the economic side of Late Antiquity, that the period of Late Antiquity saw states grow on their own economic networks, while others saw decline.
