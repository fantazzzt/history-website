Understanding Antiquity

Spanning 284-750 CE

Europe after the Fall of the Western Roman Empire, 476

It wasn't more than half a century when Peter Brown transformed the history surrounding the Late Roman Empire. His academic work reexamined the discussion surrounding this period, notably the seminal work 'The History of the Decline and Fall of the Roman Empire' by Edward Gibbon. Rather than the previous idea of massive barbarian hordes tearing apart the empire with the destructive power of Christianity, Peter Brown finds powerful transformations undertaking the Roman Empire that isn't exactly sudden or as harsh as previously led on to be. Additionally, these transformations do not have 'good' or 'bad' connotations but are different expressions in certain areas, such as art, religious work, and buildings.

As such, the period of Late Antiquity, a period of transitions between the old classical history and the early medieval era, is roughly defined between the start of Diocletions reign and ends with the Arab invasions of the Sassian and Byzantine Empires. Notably, this includes the Fall of the Western Roman Empire in 476 CE, signaling continuity carrying over before and after the fall. Additionally, these "barbarians" have been reexamined as being strong political entities that carry a variety of ethnic and cultural backgrounds, rather than one homogeneous unit.

With this website, I hope to showcase some of these developments in the historical field and dispel previous notions of how to understand the Roman Empire.
